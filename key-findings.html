Key Findings

This research examines explanatory and predictive analyses of rent pricing, focusing on the 2017 merger of LRO with RealPage/YieldStar. The explanatory/causal inference section applies Difference-in-Difference to compare multifamily rent indices against single-family benchmarks nationwide, with robustness checks using synthetic control regression and alternative rent measures. It also explores geographic differences based on RealPage/YieldStar and LRO adoption. The predictive/ML section tests whether observed listing-level prices align better with a “no collusion” or “collusion” assumption, using the White House’s elasticity-based pricing model. The study leverages metro-level rent indices and individual listings to refine predictions. Additional correlation tests examine whether RealPage users price rents higher than non-users when controlling for factors like building age and metro area. Identifying RealPage buildings involves using the "HeroSource" variable and scraping company websites. Obstacles include justifying causal claims and demonstrating collusion conclusively.
